\title{Tensor-network simulations of bound
states in perturbed quantum
Ising chains}
\author{Jannis Lutz, Anil Jean Pekgöz, Pavle Smiljanić}
\date{\today}
\begin{abstract}
Tensor network methods, in particular Matrix Product States (MPS), 
provide a powerful framework for studying quantum many-body systems that fulfill the area law. 
In this work, we investigate the transverse-field Ising model using the Time-Evolving Block Decimation (TEBD) algorithm. 
By performing both imaginary- and real-time evolution, we characterize ground-state properties and compute dynamical correlation functions, 
with the ultimate goal of extracting the dynamic structure factor $S(k, \omega)$.
\end{abstract}
